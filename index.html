Good to know

Money & tipping

  • Card/contactless works everywhere, even market stalls — you barely need cash. Exception: Kung Fu Burger, cash only.
  • Restaurants usually add a 12.5% "service charge" — that is the tip. No tipping at pubs, bars or street food; round up taxis if you like.
  • Prices always include VAT — the tag price is what you pay.

Getting around

  • No tickets: tap in and out with contactless card/phone on Tube & Overground (buses: tap in only, flat fare). One card per person; fares cap daily (~£9, zones 1–2).
  • Stand on the right on escalators — locals are serious about this.
  • Traffic comes from the right — look right first (it's painted on the road).
  • Get Citymapper — beats Google Maps for London transport. Avoid the Tube 08:00–09:30 & 17:00–18:30.
  • Night Tube runs Fri & Sat nights on several lines — useful after Wembley.

Pubs & food

  • Order and pay at the bar — no table service in pubs. Grab a table first; rounds are the custom.
  • Last orders come early — many pubs close ~23:00.
  • Drinking age 18, "Challenge 25" is common — carry photo ID even if obviously older.
  • Licensed places must give free tap water — ask for "tap water".
  • Popular spots book out; queueing for walk-ins (Dishoom, Gloria) is normal and moves fast.
  • Cheap-lunch hack: supermarket meal deal (Tesco/Sainsbury's/Boots) ~£4–5.

Practical

  • UK plugs are Type G, 230 V — bring adapters.
  • Check your mobile plan — since Brexit the UK is excluded from many EU roaming bundles.
  • Emergency: 999 (112 works too). Pharmacies: Boots or any green cross.
  • The queue is sacred — join the back, never push in.
  • Layers + compact umbrella — rain shows up unannounced even in summer.
  • Sunday shop hours are short by law (bigger shops ~12:00–18:00).
